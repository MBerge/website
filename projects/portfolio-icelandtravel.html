Iceland Travel — UI & Frontend

A website designed for travel information on the mobile. Itineraries and package holiday bookings were provided by the website in a clear and carefully constructed manner for the user.

Client
Iceland Travel
Timeline
2 months · 2012-2013
My role
UI and Frontend developer
Deliverables
UI/HTML/CSS

Overview

Holidays at the fingertips

Iceland Travel´s clients rely on their local knowledge to care for them in the best way possible. They have solved all manner of problems over the years, and have developed smooth and simple processes that stop most of them cropping up. They required a website that could do the same and my role was to build it.

Whether it was a week stay or a weekend stopover, the website had to work quickly and provide a detailed level of information that just worked. The design had to answer questions before they were asked and make the entire process of booking easy. It had to work both on the desktop and on the mobile.

A view of the mobile package holiday screen.
Detail panel for the desktop with booking elements.

Build Process

Modular elements

Building blocks are the fastest and most efficient way to create and maintain websites. In this site, I ensured that the addition of elements were combinations of easily updated parts to quickly build the site. Using programmatic SCSS I was able to key in variables that were centralised and accessible. Using a standardized grid system, the site was responsive to the mobile environment and to the desktop.

UX application
Iceland Travel deals in package holidays and all the information connected to such a package made for an convenient grouping. I ensured that the most important information featured in Hero elements and kept further detail to subsequent pages. Price, location and imagery all worked together for a clean and intriguing peek into what was in store.
Prototype
Developing the working prototype in a private server meant the client could view the work as it progressed and make comments where necessary. The mockup was expressly built with mobile-first principles in mind and accessibilty was a prime concern. The full HTML prototype was delivered on time and ready for further technical developement.
Testing
As the prototype progressed, the client was keen to input into the design and the workings of the website. Tests were setup on a weekly basis to ensure a structured feedback loop so the work progressed without delay. All changes were logged and addressed speedily

Outcomes

Bookings made simple

As a primarily mobile application, the challenges of providing a workable app were successfully overcome and provided users with an enjoyable experience. Feedback showed that ease of use was particularly appreciated and the look gave the traveller a sense of Iceland's opporunties.
